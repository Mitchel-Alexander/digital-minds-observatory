Digital Minds Engagement Index · the public record · as of July 2026

How frontier AI developers engage with digital-minds questions

Researchers increasingly ask whether advanced AI systems could be conscious, have welfare, or warrant moral consideration. This page records how the three largest frontier AI developers engage with those questions in their formal public documents — system and model cards, safety frameworks, model specifications, and welfare statements. Every claim on this page traces to a verbatim quoted passage, or to a documented search that found none.

This is a record of public engagement, not a score: it registers the presence of documented engagement, never its adequacy or sincerity — and “no public evidence” describes the public record, not what happens inside a company.

Data & method

How this was made

  • 30 formal documents (2019–2026), registry-defined, captured July 2026
  • Independently coded twice, by coders from different model families
  • Status-level agreement 0.75; no found↔absent disagreements in 200 dual-coded cells
  • Every quote mechanically verified verbatim against its source
  • Every absence claim carries its documented search list

    • Use this data

  • Indicator registry (CSV) · JSON
  • Per-cell status (JSON)
  • OpenAI verdicts · Google verdicts · Anthropic search verdicts
  • Methodology: DMEI Partner Draft 4 (on request)

    • Citing & updates

  • Cite as: Digital Minds Engagement Index, pilot evidence, July 2026 — with the cell permalink for specific claims
  • Designed for quarterly updates; each edition version-stamped against the registry
  • Developers are offered a right of reply before publication
